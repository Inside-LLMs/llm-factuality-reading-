# DoLa: Decoding by Contrasting Layers Improves Factuality in LLMs (ICLR 2024)
*ICLR 2024*

**Alias:** 后层打假

## 1. What problem does the paper solve?

Large language models often hallucinate during generation.
The paper asks whether decoding alone can improve factuality without:

- fine-tuning
- retrieval or external knowledge (RAG)
- extra training

The goal is to make decoding rely more on the model's later-layer factual knowledge.

## 2. Key observation

The authors observe that different transformer layers encode different signals:

- early layers: syntax, surface form, fluent plausibility
- later layers: semantics, factual knowledge

In a factual error case (e.g. "Seattle" vs "Olympia"), the wrong token may
have high probability in early layers, while the correct token gains probability
in later layers.

This suggests that factual knowledge is injected gradually into deeper layers.

## 3. Core idea: contrast late and early layers

DoLa computes layer-wise next-token distributions from intermediate hidden states.
For layer j it defines:

```text
q_j(x_t | x_<t) = softmax(head(h_t^(j)))
```

Then it contrasts the final layer distribution q_N with an earlier layer q_M:

```text
score(x_t) = log(q_N(x_t) / q_M(x_t))
```

The final output distribution is:

```text
p_hat(x_t | x_<t) = softmax(log(q_N / q_M))
```

Tokens whose probability rises from the early layer to the final layer are
boosted, while tokens that are only plausible in early layers are suppressed.

## 4. Dynamic premature layer selection

DoLa does not fix M in advance.
It chooses the premature layer M from a candidate set by maximizing the Jensen-
Shannon divergence between q_N and q_j:

```text
M = argmax_{j in J} JSD(q_N || q_j)
```

The idea is that the layer with the greatest divergence from the final layer
is the one where later factual updates are most visible.

## 5. Adaptive Plausibility Constraint (APC)

To avoid amplifying low-quality tokens, DoLa restricts the contrast to tokens
that are still plausible under the final layer.

Only tokens satisfying:

```text
q_N(x_t) >= alpha * max_w q_N(w)
```

are kept; others receive a score of -infinity.
The paper uses:

```text
alpha = 0.1
```

This keeps the method focused on reasonable next-token candidates.

## 6. Inference flow

1. Generate hidden states for selected layers.
2. Compute q_j for each candidate premature layer.
3. Compute JSD(q_N || q_j).
4. Select M = argmax JSD.
5. Compute contrast scores log(q_N / q_M).
6. Apply softmax to get the final token distribution.

## 7. Experimental results

### TruthfulQA

- LLaMA-7B original MC2 = 40.6
- DoLa MC2 = 63.8
- improvement = +23.2 points

### Open-ended generation

- Truthfulness improves by 12–17%
- no RAG, no fine-tuning, no additional training required

### Chain-of-thought reasoning

- StrategyQA improves by about 1–4%
- GSM8K improves by about 2%

This indicates DoLa can help reasoning as well as factuality.

### Latency

Additional inference cost is only about 1.01× to 1.08×.

## 8. Difference from Contrastive Decoding

Contrastive Decoding uses two models:

```text
Expert - Amateur
```

DoLa uses the same model with two layers:

```text
LateLayer - EarlyLayer
```

This removes the need for a second model, extra training, or extra
memory for a companion model.

## 9. Take-away

DoLa shows that layer-wise contrast inside a single model can amplify factual
signals from later layers and reduce hallucination, with minimal overhead.
